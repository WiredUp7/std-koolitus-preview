Monitoring dashboard user guide

A step-by-step overview of everything you see and can do as a client: from logging in to the dashboard, the companies you monitor, the product catalogue, services, payments and reports. Each button explains what it does and where you see the result.

The Look360 monitoring dashboard brings everything we watch for you into one place – competitors' prices and stock, Facebook posts, the health of your own online shop. You don't need to install anything: you log in, see the state of play at a glance, and get the important changes by email every morning too. This guide walks through every view and button.

1 What the dashboard is

The monitoring dashboard is your private workspace at an address where you log in with your email and password. There you see every company you monitor – both your own online shop and your competitors – and every change the system has caught: a price drop, a stock-out, a new Facebook post, an online-shop error.

Everything works on colour logic. The same three colours recur everywhere – on the dashboard, on the company card, in the table:

Green – all goodNo changes, or everything is as expected. Nothing for you to do.
Amber – needs attentionA warning: e.g. a competitor's price change, a stock-out or outdated data.
Red – errorA data source was temporarily unreachable. It usually recovers on its own or the operator checks it.
No need to watch your inbox

We also send the most important changes in a single email every morning. You open the dashboard when you want to see more or change something.

2 Logging in

  1. Open Log in – either from the top corner of the home page, from the top of this guide, or from the direct link we sent you.
  2. Enter your username – this is your email address (e.g. you@company.com).
  3. Enter your password and press Sign in
  • Forgot your password? – from the link below we'll email you a link to set a new password.
  • Don't have an account yet? – the Register button takes you to the pricing on the home page, where you can put together a package and sign up.
  • Back to the home page – the top link takes you back to look360.eu.
Good to know

Your account is linked to your company. After logging in you see your company name on the green bar at the top left – so you know you're in the right account.

3 Menu and overview

Every page has the same frame: a left-hand menu, a top bar and the content in the middle.

Left-hand menu

  • Monitoring – the main dashboard with all the companies you monitor and the findings.
  • Services – the services you've ordered, the monthly fee and the option to order more.
  • Repricing – appears only if you have ordered the price repricing service.
  • At the bottom is your username, the role Client and the log-out icon (out the door).

Top bar

  • On the left, the page title (e.g. your company name) and, in fine print below, when it was last updated.
  • On the right, the dark/light toggle (sun/moon) and the action buttons for that particular page.
  • On mobile, the ☰ button in the top corner opens the left-hand menu.

4 The monitoring dashboard

This is your home view. At the top you'll find the main buttons; below is the overview and the list of companies.

Top-bar buttons

  • Add company – add a new company to monitor (see chapter 5).
  • Services – open the services and monthly-fee view.
  • Refresh view – re-reads the latest data and refreshes the screen. It does not scrape sites, it just refreshes the view.
  • Re-scan everything – appears when you monitor online shops or Facebook. It runs real scans (scrapes the sites) and takes a few minutes. You can do this once per day.

How does monitoring work? – the help bar

At the top of the dashboard there is an expandable bar How does monitoring work? Click it to read a short colour explanation. The first time, when you don't yet have any companies, it is open.

Warnings trend

The chart shows how the number of warnings has changed over the most recent monitoring runs – whether things are settling down or need more attention.

Summary

On the right are four numbers and a status line:

Companies monitoredHow many companies you are monitoring right now.
Active warningsHow many things need attention (amber).
Sources with errorsHow many data sources are currently unreachable (red).
Outdated dataHow many companies' data has gone stale and needs refreshing.

Tabs, views and export

  • Tabs All My company Competitors – filter the list.
  • Table / Cards – switch how the list looks. The table is compact; the cards show each company's channels straight away.
  • Sort – order by warnings, findings, health or name.
  • Export CSV – downloads the visible table as a file you can open in Excel.

Company table

Each row is one monitored company. Click a row to open that company's full view. Columns:

CompanyThe name and whether it's your company or a competitor.
StatusAll good · N warnings · Error · Outdated / Not run
ChannelsHow many sources are monitored for this company (web, Facebook, etc.).
FindingsHow many changes were found in the last monitoring run.
WarningsHow many findings need attention.
FreshnessWhen this company's data was last updated.
HealthAn overall status bar 0–100% (green good, amber medium, red weak).

Companies you've added

If you've added a company yourself, you see its status in a separate section. The possible states are:

  • Pending – awaiting the operator's confirmation.
  • Building… / AI detecting… – the online-shop reader is being set up automatically.
  • Fee pending – the data is ready, the monthly fee needs confirming (see chapter 6).
  • Rejected – the request was declined (the reason is given).
  • Active – the company is being monitored.

Facebook monitoring

A separate card shows the latest Facebook posts of the competitors you monitor, with the newest and new ones highlighted. Clicking a post takes you to the original on Facebook.

All findings and report history

  • All findings – all the changes from the last monitoring run in one list. The Warnings only checkbox hides the calm part.
  • Report history – earlier summary reports. Clicking opens an archived report.

5 Adding a company

On the dashboard, press Add company. A simple form opens.

  1. Company name – start typing and pick a company from the Estonian business register (or type the name in by hand).
  2. Website – the name alone is enough, without the https:// at the start, e.g. weekend.ee.
  3. If you wish, switch on monitor the online shop's prices and stock and/or monitor Facebook too.
  4. Press Add company to monitor

Online-shop monitoring

If you choose online-shop monitoring, the system tries to read the shop's products itself. If it succeeds, monitoring starts automatically; otherwise the operator reviews it. You can also open a keyword filter: type a keyword and press Enter or + to add a chip (e.g. lawnmower). Leave it empty and we monitor all products.

Pricing model

Online-shop monitoring costs €5 / month per up to 2000 monitored products. Once the system has read the shop's products, you choose which categories to monitor yourself – that determines the number of products and the monthly fee. We show the exact amount and ask you to confirm before we open the data.

Facebook monitoring

Enter the Facebook page address – the page name or address is enough (e.g. facebook.com/konkurent or simply competitor). If the page isn't on the monitoring list yet, it goes to the operator for confirmation.

6 Company view and product catalogue

Clicking a company name on the dashboard opens its full view: all channels, findings and – for an online shop – the whole product catalogue.

Header buttons

  • Choose categories – choose which shop categories to monitor (see chapter 7).
  • Open site – opens the competitor's online shop in a new window.

Status messages

At the top of the view there may be a message: awaiting the operator's confirmation, the scraper is being built, detecting categories, automatic setup failed (the operator reviews it) or the request was rejected.

Confirming the monthly fee

Once the shop's products are read, you see a calculator: number of products, tiers (× up to 2000 products), base price €5, any surcharge and the total monthly fee. Until you confirm, the catalogue is shown as a sample and is blurred. The options are:

  • Pay and open the data – we send you to a secure payment gateway (bank link or card); the data opens immediately.
  • Pay by bank-transfer invoice – we email you an invoice; the content opens once the payment arrives.
  • Choose categories – reduce the products so the monthly fee drops.

Confirming Facebook monitoring works the same way (€1/month).

Product catalogue

  • Search – type a product name to filter the list.
  • Categories – click a category to open its subcategories and products.
  • Stock – next to each product: in stock, out of stock or unknown.
  • Price – for a sale price you see the regular price struck through and the sale price in red.
  • The external link (arrow) takes you to the product in the shop.
  • Clicking a product row opens the price and stock history chart: the current price and discount, the regular price as a dashed line, stock over time as a bar and a legend.

7 Choosing categories

The Choose categories button opens a list of all the shop's categories. The fewer products you choose, the lower the monthly fee.

  1. Tick the categories you want to monitor. Select all, Clear selection and the search help.
  2. Watch the calculator below: selected products → tiers (× up to 2000) → the monthly fee updates instantly.
  3. Press Save selection

You can change the selection again at any time – the monthly fee adjusts accordingly.

8 Services and monthly fee

From the Services menu you see everything you pay for and you can order more.

  • Your total monthly fee – the big number at the top. The amount comes from all your active services. Next to it is the date of your next billing.
  • Paid – awaiting activation – orders whose initial fee is paid and which the operator will switch on shortly.
  • Your services – all active services (in green), including competitors' Facebook and web monitoring. Cancel ends a service; competitor monitoring is managed from Add company (the Manage button).
  • Order more – each service shows a price in €/month and an initial fee. Press Order, confirm in the dialog and pay the initial fee. For special tasks there is a Request a quote button.
  • Packages – order several services at once with the same payment flow.
How the initial fee is calculated

When you order a new service you immediately pay a pro-rata initial fee – only up to the next billing date. The operator then switches the service on, and from then on all services are billed together.

9 How payment works

Every payment starts with a confirmation dialog stating the amount and what you are paying for. You can always Cancel.

  • Paying immediately, we send you to a secure payment gateway – you pay by bank link or card, and the data/service opens right after payment.
  • By bank-transfer invoice – we email you an invoice. The content opens in full once the payment has arrived.
  • The initial fee is always pro-rata up to the next billing date; after that the full monthly fee applies.
  • Billing happens for all services at once, on the anniversary of your sign-up day.

10 Repricing

The Repricing menu item is visible only if you have ordered the price repricing service. There you see competition-based price recommendations for your own shop.

  • Recommendations table – product, current price, recommended price, difference and reason.
  • Regenerate calculates the recommendations afresh; Rules and History show the settings and earlier changes.
  • When the service is activated, you can write a recommendation to your WooCommerce shop with one click; Leave as is keeps the price and ignores the product for the chosen number of days, Skip skips the recommendation.
Safely

Look360 switches on writing price changes to the shop together with you. By default everything is a dry run (nothing is changed) until we agree on live mode.

11 Reports

  • Daily morning email – the most important changes reach your (and your agreed contacts') inbox automatically.
  • Real-time alerts – the most critical things (e.g. a competitor's price drop, an online-shop error, a stock-out) reach you straight away; the rest is gathered into the morning report.
  • Report history – on the dashboard you can open any earlier summary report and see what changed then.

12 Dark mode and mobile

  • Dark / light mode – the top-bar toggle (sun/moon) switches the theme. Your choice is remembered.
  • On mobile – the menu is hidden behind the ☰ button in the top corner; the content adapts to the screen width.
  • Logging out – the out-the-door icon next to your username at the bottom left.
